What is Emotional Intelligence and how does it differ from IQ

The traditional view of intelligence is often thought of as the ability to learn from experiences, recognize problems, and then solve those problem using prior learned knowledge. Intelligence is generally measured or rather estimated with IQ tests, where participant scores in various tests are compared to the median average of 100, Abilities tested include logic, reasoning, working memory, math and language skill, visual and spatial processing, ability to understand and concentrate.

Emotional intelligence arose from observations and complaints that the traditional view of intelligence generally looked at with IQ, doesn’t represent the full facet of human intelligence, but only a narrow look at cognitive abilities. Complaints often centred around highly gifted individuals, whose ability and understanding of their talents far exceeded average, but those individuals would often score average in abilites IQ tests would look at. An often-used example would be Mozart, whose musical ability and knowledge exceeded his contemporaries and the majority of people who have ever lived, but’s it claimed that he was poor in other abilities like math. Is it fair to label Mozart unintelligent? A prominent theory which arose from this thinking was Howard Garners Frames of mind, a theory of multiple intelligences, which identified 7 different areas of intelligence (and suggested a possible two more naturalist and existential);

  • Logical-mathmatical
  • Linguistic
  • Spatial
  • Musical
  • Bodily-Kinesthetic
  • Intrapersonal
  • Interpersonal

Another term used to explain different kinds of intelligence is Emotional intelligence, which combines two of Garners areas interpersonal and intrapersonal. Emotional intelligence seeks to describe the ability of a person to identify, asses and control emotions in themselves and in others. Goleman describes emotion intelligence With 5 different attributes

The intrapersonal

•Understanding your emotions, or self-awareness

Ability to recognise your own emotions as they are happening to you, knowledge of how these emotions influence your behaviour and thoughts. Knowledge of your strengths and limitations and Self-confidence

• Ability to regulate your emotions, or self-management

Ability to manage emotions and control the impulsive behaviours that are caused by those emotions. Trustworthiness, maintaining personal standards of honesty and integrity.

•Motivation

Being able to find and set fulfilling goals in life while being able to managing internal resources to reach those goals. Ability to focus and be self-disciplined

The interpersonal

•Empathy, or social awareness

Understanding emotions, anxieties and needs in other people, with better skills in picking up on non-verbal signals, the easier it is to send corresponding reassuring signals back. Recognising power dynamics or political structures in groups of people. Sensing and anticipation of areas of growth in other people

•Social skills, or relationship management

Maintenance and development of relationships, ability to communicate clearly, with influence or inspiration. Managing conflicts and changes.

Why is Emotional Intelligence Important

While IQ was often touted as a high indicator of success in work and life, research has found it isn’t always the case, not every high IQ scoring person was destined for success. This can be crudely demonstrated with the typical typecast of a brilliant but socially inept person, without the ability to navigate and function in a highly social world success will be very limited, Not only in the workplace but also in personal lives. EQ is a lot more dynamic than IQ, it is easier to practice and improve EQ than compared to IQ.

Studies have also shown that individuals with higher levels of EQ, compared to those with low EQ perform better in life regardless of IQ. This shows that neither EQ or IQ alone are sufficient indicators for a successful life, but rather parts of a complex structure of influences.

With life being such a social construct, improving your ability to navigate and function within is imperative. By working on EQ you are able to improve your quality of life, build stronger and more productive relationships with friends, family and colleagues, all while strengthening your mental fortitude.
