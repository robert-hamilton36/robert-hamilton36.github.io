'Identity, Values and Strengths'

I really struggled with the question of my Values, Identity and Strengths, I felt like it’s something I just implicitly knew already. I felt like I had a good sense of who I was, even reflected on it and critiqued myself, but I really struggled to put it down into words, I wasn’t able to articulate it. Things I was able to write down were erratic, there was no flow and only vague connections between them, taken by themselves they made little sense, no picture was painted. Only I was able to know what was meant, what it related to, how the birds nest unravelled. I realised, with the help of the course material, If I couldn’t explain it to a 5 year old, I didn’t really know it. Which doesn't quite feel right.

Something that helped me start a journey of self-rediscovery or corroboration on what I felt was this quote I read:

‘When the things that you do and the way you behave match your values, life is usually good – you're satisfied and content. But when these don't align with your personal values, that's when things feel... wrong. This can be a real source of unhappiness’

It helped me find my first Value; Adventure. The times I’ve had the most fun and been the happiest have been on adventures, especially trips overseas. I find it really easy to fall into my comfort zone, avoiding things and other people that trouble you and cause discomfort, But going on big adventures often puts you in uncomfortable and unfamiliar situations. I found I hated planning and wanted to do things spur of the moment, I would wake up one morning sometimes and decide to go to a completely different country, or some smaller town, having no idea about it or even where I would sleep or eat. It seems very counter-intuitive, but I kept thinking about it and realised we only grow when we are uncomfortable, when we are challenged. Growth, it’s something I value, but I’m very bad at it.

Another Value I found was creativity. Shout out to Jo$eph for sharing his values and helping me realise and articulate this. When he first said creativity, I thought, No way that’s not me, I can’t paint, I can’t draw, my imagination has regressed massively since I was a kid, but he helped me look at it another way, with a different meaning, that is to make, or create, stuff. I really like making stuff, whether cooking or baking food or trying to make furniture, One day I’d really like to make a guitar. It helped me realise another value which is Curiosity, I found this because I often feel like I would want something for example a guitar pedal, I found out they can be very easy (relatively) to make and got really interested in that, and then created a few. I think my biggest cultural influence has been my family, my Dad’s family has a Bach where a lot of the structures there have been a diy job, and was always encouraged to create, especially with Lego’s. Another big part I remember growing up is a lot of long walks up mountains and trips to beautiful scenery.

I found out that a lot of what I see as my strengths, if I was to begin to imagine telling other people about them, I would instantly start to doubt it and find limitations to. I think my biggest limitation will be self-doubt and my confidence in myself. I might think that I’m fast learner, but then I might think, what happens when I claim that I can learn fast but begin struggling to pick up these new things. I might think, and get feedback that I’m a good listener, but then remember the times when I was to tired to make an effort or too interested in some other thoughts and ignored what was being said to me, but nodded along anyway.

Trying to remember ethical decisions I had to make, was difficult, I hope that means that by being difficult to remember any dilemma I actually had, meant that it was met with integrity, hence it not weighing on my mind heavily. Although how likely might I be to share something to the contrary Actualy I did just remember one at the supermarket, one day the wind blew my car door out of my hands and into the vehicle next to me wing mirror, bending it backwards. There was a little voice, a temptation to just leave it, or put it back in place and continue shopping. It kind of feels like the choice was obvious and already made, but I guess I would have weighed up honesty, integrity, I couldn’t just leave it and forget about it I would remember it going to bed it wouldn’t sit right to me, and empathy, How would I have felt if I had come back to my car to find the wing mirror broken or dented. I left a message for the driver, and later received a txt that everything was alright

At my previous work I often tried to mediate between two of the other managers who didn’t work well together, but their strengths were each other’s weaknesses. One was very organised and logical, they would make plans on how and who would tackle all the work, at the expense, while the other was very customer and product orientated In an attempt to keep the work culture healthy and staff moral up, I tried to get them both to stop complaining about the other to other staff members and only complain to me. I would then have tried to get them to see the others points of view. While I was able to get them to see the others pov, I was only able to get this to occur during the conversation, not any other time. Letting them blow of steam to me, often caused the conversation to be completely focused on the negative and I failed to turn it into a positive. I can see now that I tried to manipulate the conversations and viewpoints from behind the scenes, mainly to avoid further conflict by forcing a frank discussion about it. I think I should have tried to sit them together discuss the problems and realise the benefits of each other and the potential to learn from each other. Then get them to make a work plan based on each other’s strengths.
