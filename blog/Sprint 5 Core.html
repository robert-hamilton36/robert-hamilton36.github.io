Reflections

What have you learnt?

That I like to quietly think that I am quite capable and smart, I think it stems from when I was trying to feel self-worth in groups of people. I’ve also learnt that this leads me into charging head first into problems and challenges, especially when I feel like I have a good grasp of what is needed to make it work. Combining that with some curiosity, things often got out of hand quickly, lots of messy jumbled code, that was hard to follow and not very self-contained, sometimes I would leave half-finished test code with no comment on what it was for. I definitely need to work on slowing down, looking at the big picture and to plan ahead, and then reflect on what I have done. I found it was really hard to break habits and I would get distracted by other things, unless I got very invested in something, this didn’t help with me with my charging into a tech problems problem, and often lead to me working on lots of tech content at the same time, while ignoring the core content until much later, when it was harder to reflect on what you had just done because you had done so many different things. I’ve found I struggle to articulate my thoughts and was surprised to find I struggled with finding my values.

Values, empathy and self-awareness in learning and programming

If you have empathy and are empathetic with fellow students, it’s likely the learning environment will be much more open and honest where everyone will feel safe to put themselves out there in ‘vulnerable’ positions. This works well in a learning environment and accentuates people in a growth mindset. Having self-awareness and knowledge of your own values, not only helps with empathy, but also in your ability to learn. Being aware of your mindset towards learning and knowledge and knowing that when your frustrated its just a pathway to learning.
Empathy and self-awareness plays a similar role in programming when working in a team, having everyone comfortable to put their ideas out there, while also making a stronger more collaborative team where everyone is understanding and accepting on who they are working with.

Surprises from core learnings

How hard it is to implement it. Some of the stuff I thought I knew or had heard about before, but it was never something that played upon my mind, I was never able to use what I had heard about in my life, or to improve my understanding or myself and things around me. Even now I still find it hard to do something about it. Comparing to the tech side, I was able to see visible growth and increases, but with the core content I didn’t have that positive feedback of improvement to encourage me to keep it up.

Challenging aspects of core learnings

Understanding myself, was probably the most challenging. I think I probably went into it thinking I already knew and understood myself, that I knew what I was about what my values are, but trying to articulate it was trickier than I was expecting and lead me to believe I don’t know myself as well as I thought.

Why focus on core learning for web development bootcamp

A large part of working successfully in a group or organisation comes from our core skills, having a solid foundation in interpersonal and intrapersonal abilities will allow us to better understand ourselves, and understand, connect and empathize with other people which leads to thriving in a collective, collaborative work setting, which will make us more desirable and competent employees

Was it a waste of time?

No, From my study and learnings around the core learnings I can definitely see their benefits, and why it isn’t a waste of time. From a different point of view, looking at myself and how it has affected me so far, the improvements are not very visible or quantifiable. While it is still early days, it’s hard to be really enthusiastic about my progress.




Bootcamp

What is your long-term goal and/or career pathway?

I guess my goal is to find happiness, with the hope that this will lead to a challenging but rewarding career that will feed into a work-life balance. I’m not sure if I want to necessarily focus more on web development but more would rather use the skills in more general software development. I’m too focused on myself to have any thoughts on what sort of impact I would like to make, so I guess an impact on myself.

Biggest strength and limitations in bootcamp

My biggest strength may be my curiosity and desire to learn. While my limitations will be my self-confidence and my ability to put myself out there, especially when I’m struggling

Biggest non-technical challenge at bootcamp

I think balancing my workload for 9 weeks, keeping myself driven and making sure I don’t burn out, and continuing to exercise will be challenging. Also meeting, learning to work with and finding where a fit in with a large group of people

Which Human Skills would I like developed

Probably my EQ, I would like to see my self-confidence improve along as my ability to empathize and connect with others, to work well and collaborate with groups of people. As well as working on my intrapersonal skills and my ability to reflect on myself.
